Our Events

  • Hackathon

    Hackathon invites participants to display their web designing skills and creative thinking skills with their paired partners. The event would be 2 hour coding challenge, after a brief workshop of 1 hour

    An exquisite display of science and technology, Where participants showcase their scientific temperament and problem-solving skills to solve real-world problems.

  • Creativa

    Creativa invites students from grades 9-12 to showcase their creativity and design skills by creating posters using various images on a topic given on the spot.

  • Shark Tank

    The "Shark Tank" event invites participants to pitch their innovative business ideas to a panel of judges. Teams will present their concepts, demonstrate market understanding, and answer challenging questions.

  • Bullseye

    The event focuses on the shooting skills of participants and their accuracy to hit the targets. Standard competition norms are to be followed. Further deliberation would be done with the respected department allowing smooth participation in the event.

  • Number Quest

    This exciting scavenger hunt engages students in solving maths-related riddles, puzzles, and problems, leading them to hidden objects or specific locations. Each solved problem reveals a new clue, guiding participants through a sequence of challenges until they reach the final destination.

  • Resolutions

    A display of the historical significance of events that have shaped our future through a stage play manner in which students will design and create their own stage plays featuring key historical events of India

  • Chronicles

    Participants will produce an engaging news video report. Showcasing their skills in reporting, capturing and compelling narratives. This event celebrates the fusion of words and images in a collaborative environment with cross schools teams.

  • RoboRun

    An accelerated clash of the fastest robots through a series of challenging obstacles. Students will have to steer their contraptions and finish the course in the least time possible.

  • Arguement Arena

    This debate competition for grades 9-12 challenges students to articulate and defend their positions on various topics, honing their critical thinking and public speaking skills. The teams would be formed on the spot by the organizers.

  • Cine Sync

    Participants will shoot footage on the event's first day and the overall atmosphere. They will then edit and produce a creative music video that encapsulates the spirit and energy of the event. This challenge combines filmmaking skills with artistic vision to tell the story of the day through a dynamic and engaging video.

  • Salvation

  • Choral Music

  • Future Fables

  • Frenetic

Eureka

Procedure
1. Participants will have to build a model and/or a display board for the project of their choice.
2. The model or display board should be pre-made or assembled at the time of the event, Participants will be provided 20 minutes to assemble their projects at the venue.
3. Participants will explain their model or display board in front of esteemed judges.
4. The judges might ask questions or provide their inputs, The responses of which will be marked separately.
5. Participants may use their creativity and knowledge to present their ideas involving scientific principle experiments and research. Participants may also use digital aids such as a screen while presenting their projects.


Guidelines
1. You are expected to present a unique and innovative idea for better marks.
2. The judges' decision will be final and shall not be questioned.
3. No additional material will be provided at the venue.
4. Participants should avoid any harmful chemicals or high electric voltages in their projects, Any such instances might cause harm to others.
5. Participants must provide a synopsis of their projects detailing the work, significance, and material used.
6. The results will be category-based for 6-8th and 9-12th.


Evaluation Criteria
Originality: 25 marks
Presentation: 20 marks
Scientific temperament: 20 marks
Significance: 20 marks
Responses: 15 marks
